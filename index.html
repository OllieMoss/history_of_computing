  • Charles Babbage

    Babbage is considered to be "father of the computer". Babbage is known for inventing the first mechanical computer, called the Difference Engine. This eventually led to more complex electronic designs, though the ideas of modern computers are to be found in Babbage's analytical engine. Babbage died before a lot of his ideas were successfully created the difference engine and the analytical engine for example. The difference engine was created in the 19th century based on his original plans but with different tolerances achievable at that time.

  • 26.12.1791 - 18.10.1871

  • Ada Lovelace

    Ada Lovlace was an English mathematician and writer, chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. She was the first to recognise that the machine had applications beyond pure calculation, and to have published the first algorithm intended to be carried out by such a machine. As a result, she is often regarded as the first computer programmer.

  • Herman Hollerith

    Herman Hollerith is widely regarded as the father of modern automatic computation hechose the punched card as the basis for storing and processing information and he built the first punched-card tabulating and sorting machines as well as the first key punch, and he founded the company that was to become IBM. Hollerith's designs dominated the computing landscape for almost 100 years.

  • Alan Turing

    Alan Turing was a British scientist and a pioneer in computer science. During World War II, he developed a machine that helped break the German Enigma code. He also laid the groundwork for modern computing and theorized about artificial intelligence.
    By the end of WWII was the only scientist that was working on the idea of a universal machine that harness the speed and reliability of electronics. This led the early development of hardware and the implementation of arithmetical functions by programming. Therefore, computer science was born.

    It is also thought that Turing had Aspergers Sydrome/was autistic

  • J.V. Atanasoff

    John Vincent Atanasoff was an American physicist and inventor known for inventing the first electronic digital computer. Atanasoff invented the first electronic digital computer in 1937 and tested successfully in 1942 in what is now the Iowa state university. In 1941 Atanasoff and one of his graduate student Clifford Berry, designed a computer that could solve 29 equations simultaneously. This was the first computer capable of storing information on it's 'main memory'.

    William Shockley, John Bardeen & Walter Brattain co-invented the transistor which is one the most influential invetions ever to be made in the history of computing it circumvented the need to use vacuum tubes and paved the way for modern electronics. They discovered how to make an electric switch with solid materials and no need for a vacuum.

  • Grace Hopper 'The first lady of software'

    Grace Hopper worked for the US navy as a computer scientist she is best known for her participation in creating the first 'all electronic digital computer', called UNIVAC. NASA used UNIVAC for the apollo missions tracking stations to communicate with astronauts. In 1953 Hopper developed the first programming languages known as COBOL. In 2016, Grace posthumously received the presidential medal of freedom.

  • Jack Kilby & Robert Noyce

    Jack Kilby & Robert Noyce independently of eachother showed that transistors, capacitors and resistors could be grouped togther on semiconductor material which became the integrated circuit also known as the micro-chip, Jack Kilby created it 1958. The integrated circuit was influential in the creation of mobile phones for example in order to make them (over time) smaller and smaller and giving the capabilities that they now have today.

  • Douglas Engelbart

    In 1964 Douglas Engelbart showed a modern computer prototype with a 'GUI' (graphical user interface), Engelbart invented the computer mouse to be used with such a machine. This marked an evolution in computing in the sense that computers didn't have be used for specific tasks for specific organisations like NASA or the military, they could be used by general public for personal use, he was also part of the development of hypertext.
